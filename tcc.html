PROJECT OVERVIEW

PROJECT SCOPE

Responsive website for a non-profit organization, functioning as a clothing library

FOCUS

User research • UX/UI design • Rebranding • Prototyping Lo-Fi/Hi-fi • Content creation • WordPress • SEO

TOOLS

Figma • FigJam • Adobe Photoshop • Adobe Illustrator • WordPress • JavaScript • JQeury

TEAM

4 ppl, Self directed with feedback from the client and peers

DURATION

4 Weeks

CHALLENGE

Even though The Clothing Club has been active for the past 5 years, their only means of communication with the target audience were Instagram and Facebook posts. Only recently, they've felt the need to fully rebrand, expand their target audience and introduce a new communication channel. And thus, they saw the necessity of having a website.

SOLUTION

We want to increase the awareness of The Clothing Club among their potential customers and develop their digital presence by cerating a website for them.

FIELD RESEARCH

The method

We created an interview guide with questions based on our findings from the desk research

We followed semi-structured interviewing method to ensure we have valid and comparable data and room for elaborating and follow-up questions to gather opinions, experiences and preferences of the target group.

Our Aim

• What are the motives of joining TCC

• Get a feel of the brand

• Ask about potential points of improvement

• Gather suggestions and ideas

We interviewed

• 4 Current members

• 2 Volunteers

• 1 Organizer

• 4 Potential customers

Findings

People join TCC for the same reasons as to why they thrift, backing up our research:

Some of the words that were used to describe TCC were: colorful, experimental, happy, sustainable, youthful

To be improved

All interviewees agreed on the necessity of having a website connected to the organization to better market the concept. The idea for general rebranding also appeared, as their digital presence is incohesive and messy.

MOODBOARD

LO-FI

The method

We used the crazy 8 method for sketching, then translated the sketches we voted for in Figma. Since this is lo-fi prototyping the focus was on functionality rather than design

Focus

The phone version was a priority, because of our client’s wishes

Following the hierarchy rules, we used different font sizes and shape dimensions. We applied the F scanning pattern easing the users’ reading

Style tile

The method

The style tile was created to serve us as a guide to ensure consistent design throughout the whole website - the colors, the shapes, the typography, icons and illustrations

Our Aim

To represent our keywords: colorful, experimental, happy, sustainable, youthful

Decisions

• Our chosen fonts fall into the sans-serif category - modern feel

• Emojis and icons used previously by the client

• Teal as a primary color represents sustainability, community and creativity

• Orange - to every CTA - contrast/energy

• Pink - a splash of playfulness and carelessness

HI-FI

The method

For the high fidelity we implemented the feedback from testing and focused on the UI, hierarchy, Gestalt principles, Jacob’s law, the Serial position and Aesthetic usability effect as well as some of Don Norman’s principles.

TESTING

Our Aim

DESKTOP

The method

After finalizing the mobile version it was time for us to develop the desktop. The process went fairly smoothly because of our reverse approach - not to overcomplicate

Challenges

The hero section in the frontpage - we couldn’t translate the image, so we went back to rethink our design and found out that the image doesn’t fully represent TCC’s values, so we changed it for one that conveys community

EXECUTION

WordPress

We did the execution of our solution in WordPress as a requirement for our project with emphasis on SEO. This was to ensure that the website will be easy to find it on the internet and maximize the number of the visitors. We included meta descriptions and alt attributes.
