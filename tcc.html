CHALLENGE

Even though The Clothing Club has been active for the past 5 years, they never fully establish their digital presence. As for now, their only means of communication with the target audience were Instagram and Facebook posts. Only in recent times, they’ve felt the need to fully rebrand, expand their target audience and introduce a new communication channel. And thus, they saw the necessity of having a website. 

The biggest challenge we might encounter during this project is adjusting the solution to the specific needs of the diverse target audience and TCC’s requirements.

SOLUTION

We want to increase the awareness of The Clothing Club among their potential customers and develop their digital presence.  To achieve that we created a website promoting our client as well as a guide for their potential content.

FIELD RESEARCH

Methods

We created an interview guide with questions based on our findings from the desk research

We followed semi-structured interviewing method to ensure we have valid and comparable data and room for elaborating and follow-up questions to gather opinions, experiences and preferences of the target group.

Our Aim

• Dive deep into understanding the motives of joining TCC

• Get a feel of the brand

• Ask about potential points of improvement

• Gather suggestions and ideas

We interviewed

• 4 Current members

• 2 Volunteers

• 1 Organizer

• 4 Potential customers

Findings

We summarized our interviews in a grid and referred to the results throughout our while ideation process.

All in all the interviews backed up our Desk research and the main reasons people join TCC are the same as why they thrift:

To be improved

Some of the words that were used to describe TCC were: colorful, experimental, happy, sustainable, youthful

All interviewees agreed on the necessity of having a website connected to the organization to better market the concept. The idea for general rebranding also appeared, as their digital presence is incohesive and messy.

Research - Sum up

Through analysis of our client, desk research, VPC and BMC, we understand the values and needs of the client.

While conducting the interviews and the analysis of our target audience, we were able to understand their behavior and needs, so now we can smoothly transition to constructing the concept of our solution

SEGMENTATION

Our Aim

By segmenting our target audience, we jump into the defining stage of the Double diamond, where we organize all accumulated data so far.

We used this marketing strategy to divide TCC’s total addressable market share into smaller groups - segments. Each segment shares common characteristics that enabled us to create targeted products, which would later be developed as different sections in our solution.

The Result

After having summarized the most important segments of our audience we can easily use them as a base for navigating our solution in the right direction.

People who:

• Want to save money

• Want to live sustainably

• Are interested in fashion and are willing to experiment with their style

Conceptualize - Sum up

By Segmenting our audience, creating personas, coming up with features, organising those features into List of requirements and testproofing it using Card sorting and User flow, we are now ready to jump into develop stage.

We have the features and it is time to give shape to them

After all the mappings were done, we analyzed the findings and had a clear idea of what our website should consist of. We came to the conclusion to focus on: A front page that shows who TCC is A lookbook that inspires potential members An original and engaging design that draws in customers and reflects on the TCC’s values

MOODBOARD

Our Aim

First, we needed to look for some inspiration for our solution. Because each member participated, we were able to get as many different ideas and “moods” as we needed to have a well rounded solution

The method

Each member collected inspo and we combined our findings into 3 moodboards with different purposes. The first one focuses on the overall aesthetic of the project, the second on the layout and the third was dedicated to the lookbook (it differs in layout in contrast to all the other pages - it is more artistic)
